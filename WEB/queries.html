Frequently Asked Queries

What is Stream Saviour?

Response: "Stream Saviour is a web interface designed to calculate the Water Quality Index (WQI) of streams and rivers. It analyzes various parameters to assess the overall health of water bodies."


How does Stream Saviour calculate WQI?

Response: "Stream Saviour calculates WQI based on inputs such as location and year. It analyzes parameters like pH, dissolved oxygen, turbidity, and pollutant concentrations to generate a comprehensive WQI score."



Can Stream Saviour predict future WQI values?

Response: "Yes, Stream Saviour has the unique capability to predict future WQI values based on historical data and trends. This feature allows users to anticipate changes in water quality over time."



Is Stream Saviour easy to use?

Response: "Yes, Stream Saviour is designed to be user-friendly. It provides a simple interface for inputting location and year and generates WQI results quickly and efficiently."



Who can benefit from using Stream Saviour?

Response: "Stream Saviour is beneficial for a wide range of users, including environmental scientists, policymakers, watershed managers, conservationists, and anyone interested in monitoring and improving water quality."



Are there any prerequisites for using Stream Saviour?

Response: "No prerequisites are required to use Stream Saviour. Users only need access to the internet and basic knowledge of the location and year they wish to analyze."



Is Stream Saviour accessible on mobile devices?

Response: "Yes, Stream Saviour is accessible on most mobile devices with internet connectivity. Users can access the web interface through their mobile browsers for on-the-go water quality assessment."



How accurate are the WQI results provided by Stream Saviour?

Response: "Stream Saviour aims to provide accurate WQI results based on scientific data and algorithms. However, the accuracy may vary depending on the quality of input data and the complexity of water quality dynamics."



Can Stream Saviour be used for educational purposes?
